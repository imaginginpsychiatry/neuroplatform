Biography

Radu S. Jasinschi obtained his B.Sc. (1975), M.Sc. (1978), and his D.Sc. (1983) in Physics at the University of Sao Paulo, Sao Paulo, Brazil. He did his post-doctoral studies in Physics between 1983 and 1984 at the University of Sao Paulo and between 1984 and 1986 at Harvard University. He was a research scientist at The Robotics Laboratory of C.M.U. in the group of Prof. Takeo Kanade from 1986 to 1988, and at the Center for Automation Research of the University of Maryland in the group of Prof. Azriel Rosenfeld from 1988 to 1992. From 1992 to 1995 he did his Ph.D. studies at the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department of C.M.U. under the supervision of Prof. Jose M. F. Moura. He taught for two semesters at the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department of C.M.U. (Fall 1975 and Spring 1976). He worked at Tektronix at the Video and Networking Division from 1996 to 1999 on MPEG-7 standardization. From 1999 to 2002 he worked at Philips Research, Briarcliff Laboratory, U.S.A., on multimedia indexing. From 2002 to 2004 he worked at Philips Research, Eindhoven Laboratory, The Netherlands, on methods to improve the visual quality of TVs. Since 2005 he has been working on medical image processing, in particular on the quantification of MRI brain images of patients with neurodegenerative diseases, such as, Alzheimer’s disease, Huntington’s disease, and epilepsy. He was an associate editor of the IEEE Transactions on Signal Processing from 1997 to 2001 (two terms) and an associate editor of the IEEE Transactions on Multimedia from 2001 to 2005 (two terms). He has been an associate editor of the Journal of Visual Image Communication and Representation since 2015. He holds 20 US patents and has 34 pending.
