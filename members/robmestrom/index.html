Biography

Rob Mestrom received the M.Sc. degree (cum laude) in Mechanical Engineering from Eindhoven University of Technology, Eindhoven, The Netherlands, in 2005.

From May 2005 till July 2009, he was a Ph.D. student with the Dynamics and Control group at the Mechanical Engineering Department of Eindhoven University of Technology. His PhD dissertation was entitled “Multiphysics modelling and experimental validation of microelectromechanical resonator dynamics”.

From July 2009 till December 2011, he has worked as a PostDoc in the Electromagnetics Group at the Electrical Engineering Department of the same University. In the PANAMA (Power Amplifiers aNd Antennas for Mobile Applications) project, he was investigating approaches for antenna beam steering by means of RF-MEMS technology and phased arrays.

From January 2012 till September 2015, he had a coordination and educational management task concerning the Bachelor Automotive program of the faculty of Electrical Engineering. He contributed to the development and implementation of the curriculum with respect to its content. Also he ensured the connection between the individual courses along the disciplines in the program, while continuously contributing to the didactic improvement.

From September 2015 onwards, his research as an assistent professor in the Electromagnetics Group focuses on multiphysics modeling with specific interest for medical applications of electromagnetics. Amongst others, this involves modeling and optimization for hyperthermia, and modeling for brain stimulation, such as Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS).
