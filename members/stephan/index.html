Biography

Stephan studied mechatronics and biomedical engineering at Stellenbosch University in South Africa, obtaining his M.Sc. in 2012. He worked for 4 years in two industries (Industrial Automation and Enterprise Mobility) before acknowledging the gravity of his calling: becoming a neuroscientist. His broad interest lies in using our increasing grasp and control of technology to help us understand and educate each other on the comparatively unknown realm of the human brain. More specifically and currently, Stephan is working on finding a way to use real time feedback from fMRI brain scans towards treating cognitive impairments in epilepsy patients.
